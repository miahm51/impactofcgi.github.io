Audience Perception of Realism

CGI has significantly changed how viewers see realism in visual media. In the past, when CGI was still developing, people could easily spot its flaws; the graphics often looked fake or unconvincing because of the limited technology available at the time. However, as CGI advanced, it achieved such high levels of photorealism that it can now make it hard to tell what's real and what's not. This improvement in CGI has shifted what audiences expect regarding realism in movies, TV shows, and video games (Louis, 2014).

One major change in how audiences view CGI is its ability to create incredibly realistic environments, objects, and even human characters. This idea is often referred to as the “uncanny valley,” which shows how viewers can feel both intrigued and uneasy about CGI characters that are almost human but not quite there. This effect is especially noticeable in movies that use hyper-realistic CGI for human figures, like The Polar Express or Beowulf, where the almost lifelike faces and movements can lead to mixed feelings among the audience. The uncanny valley effect highlights the tricky balance between realism and believability that CGI needs to strike to keep viewers engaged (Louis, 2014).

Creativity and Artistic Freedom

CGI has opened amazing opportunities for creativity and artistic expression in visual media. It enables creators to turn their wildest ideas into reality without the constraints that come with traditional filmmaking. Thanks to CGI, filmmakers, game designers, and animators can dream up and design entire worlds, unique creatures, and stunning effects that would be too difficult or expensive to create in real life. This newfound freedom has expanded the range of stories that can be told and the ways they can be visually portrayed.

Anime has also gained a lot from the freedom that CGI provides. Recently, animation studios have started using CGI to produce complex action sequences, detailed character designs, and rich backgrounds that would take a lot of time to create with just hand-drawn methods. A great example is Demon Slayer, which uses CGI to enhance its colorful and energetic fight scenes, mixing traditional animation with digital techniques to make the visuals even more impressive. This method gives anime creators the chance to introduce new ideas and styles to the genre, reaching a level of excitement and detail that was hard to achieve before (Louis, 2014).
