Introduction

Computer-Generated Imagery, or CGI, is all about making visual content using digital technology. It's widely used in movies, video games, ads, and various other media like anime. In the beginning, CGI was basic, with simple graphics like wireframes and straightforward animations, mostly because of the limited tech available back then. But over the years, CGI has changed a lot, evolving from flat images to incredibly realistic environments that look just like the real world. This development has made CGI an essential part of the entertainment industry, as well as in advertising and virtual experiences, allowing creators to showcase detailed and vibrant scenes that really expand what we can see visually.

Purpose of the Review

CGI has come a long way since its beginning in iconic movies Jurassic Park (Pearson,2023). Statement

CGI has significantly changed modern media in two main ways: it has made visuals more realistic than ever before and has also opened new path for creativity. This technology allows creators to produce stunning images that were previously unimaginable. In this review, we will explore how these two effects have transformed visual media, with CGI raising the bar for what audiences expect in terms of realism and expanding the creative options for artists and storytellers.
